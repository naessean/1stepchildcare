Infant Program

Both of our Infant Classrooms are licensed for seven children with two educators, with the age range spanning from 2 months to 9 months in Infant I and 9 months to 15 months in Infant II.

Program Details

Children foster attachment and a sense of security through one-on-one interaction with caregivers throughout the caregiving routine.

Throughout the day, children engage in physical activity and discovery through play on the floor with a variety of toys and materials that are age appropriate and stimulating.

Our Infant Educators also engage infants through play, singing, reading books, sensory activities, and daily walks to promote socialization and language.

Our Infant Teachers interact with children at their level, as eye contact and recognition are important in an infant's life and throughout their development.

We believe that each child is a unique individual that learns at his or her own pace, and quarterly reports are given to parents throughout the first year to monitor each child's individual development.

We prioritize providing a safe, nurturing and loving environment where children feel welcome to learn and grow with the guidance and support of our teachers.

Caregiving Routine One-on-one interaction to build security and attachment
Daily Enrichment Play, sensory, songs, books, and walks
Development Focus Eye contact, recognition, socialization, and language
Family Updates Quarterly reports during the first year

What infants experience

  • Discovery through age-appropriate toys and materials
  • Sensory activities and floor play
  • Singing and reading books
  • Daily walks (weather permitting)
  • Guidance and support in a safe, loving environment
